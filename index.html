About

I am a Predoctoral Fellow (SPARKS) at the Indian Institute of Science, working with Prof. Sumit K. Mandal. I am pursuing a B.Tech in ECE (Quantum Tech) at PES University (Class of 2026).

At IISc, I study how spectral graph theory, particularly expander constructions, can optimize qubit connectivity and reduce circuit compilation overhead. Our work on efficient coupling map design using Near-Ramanujan Graphs with theoretical guarantees to maximize fidelity was recognized with the Bronze Medal in the ACM Student Research Competition at MICRO 2025.

I am broadly interested in quantum complexity theory and algorithmic aspects of near-term quantum computation. For my undergraduate thesis, I worked on making the training and learning of variational quantum algorithms more feasible on photonic quantum computers, with Prof. Kaustav Bhowmick.
