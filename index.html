Prateek P. Kulkarni

I am a Predoctoral Fellow (SPARKS) in the Department of Computer Science and Automation at the Indian Institute of Science, advised by Prof. Sumit K. Mandal. My research interests lie in quantum computing architecture, spectral graph theory, and their applications to quantum error correction and hardware design.

My work focuses on the theoretical foundations of quantum processor design, particularly in optimizing coupling maps (graph structures that represent qubit connectivity). I study how spectral properties of these graphs affect quantum circuit fidelity and error correction thresholds. Currently, I am investigating the use of near-Ramanujan graphs for optimal quantum coupling map design and developing analytical models for quantum processor fidelity that are validated on actual quantum hardware.
