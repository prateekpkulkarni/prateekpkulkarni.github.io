Prateek P. Kulkarni

I am a Predoctoral Fellow (SPARKS) in the Department of Computer Science and Automation at the Indian Institute of Science, where I work with Prof. Sumit K. Mandal. My research interests include quantum computer architecture and resource-aware hardware design.

My current work focuses on the theoretical foundations of quantum processor design, with an emphasis on coupling-map optimization. In particular, I study the use of near-Ramanujan graph constructions for qubit connectivity and develop analytical fidelity models, which are validated on real quantum hardware.

Previously, my undergraduate thesis investigated programmable photonic processors for variational quantum algorithms, under the supervision of Prof. Kaustav Bhowmick. I am pursuing a B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering, with a specialization in VLSI and Quantum Technologies, at PES University (Class of 2026).

Research Interests

My specific research interests are:

• Quantum architecture and coupling map design

• Quantum error correction and fault tolerance

• Randomized algorithms for quantum systems

• Hardware–software co-design for quantum processors

• Resource estimation and quantifying quantum “advantage”

Our work on efficient coupling map design using expander graphs was recognized with the Bronze Medal in the ACM Student Research Competition at MICRO 2025, in the undergraduate category.
