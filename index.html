Prateek P. Kulkarni

I am a Predoctoral Fellow (SPARKS) in the Department of Computer Science and Automation at the Indian Institute of Science, advised by Prof. Sumit K. Mandal. My research interests lie in quantum computing architecture, spectral graph theory, and their applications to quantum error correction and hardware design.

At IISc, my work focuses on the theoretical foundations of quantum processor design, especially the optimization of coupling maps. I am currently investigating the use of near-Ramanujan graphs for optimal qubit connectivity and developing analytical fidelity models validated on real quantum hardware.

My undergraduate thesis explored programmable photonic processors for variational quantum algorithms, advised by Prof. Kaustav Bhowmick. I am pursuing my B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering with a specialization in VLSI and Quantum Technologies at PES University (class of 2026).
