About me

I am a predoctoral fellow at IISc under the SPARKS Programme, advised by Prof. Sumit K. Mandal.

At IISc, my work involves use of tools from theoretical computer science to analyze and inform better quantum hardware design, particularly around qubit connectivity and circuit compilation. Our work on coupling map design received the ACM SRC Bronze Medal at MICRO 2025 (Undergraduate category).

I am concurrently pursuing a B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering (Quantum Technology track) at PES University (Class of 2026).

Research interests
I am broadly interested in quantum algorithms and complexity — what quantum computers can and cannot do efficiently. This includes quantum speedups for combinatorial and algebraic problems, quantum learning and property testing of Boolean functions and quantum states, and the interplay between coding theory and complexity (list-decoding, local testability, pseudorandomness) in the quantum setting.

Research ∈ {Quantum Algorithms, Quantum Learning Theory, Quantum Complexity Theory, Analysis of Boolean Functions, Property Testing}

If you think our interests overlap, I’d love to hear from you — feel free to reach out by email.
