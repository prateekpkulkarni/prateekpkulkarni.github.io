Final year undergraduate pursuing BTech in ECE with specialization in VLSI and Quantum Technology. Research interests span quantum computing architectures, advanced memory systems, and electronic design automation for next-generation computing platforms.

Research

My research focuses on quantum computing systems at multiple scales, from distributed architectures to algorithm optimization. I am currently working with Prof. Sumit K. Mandal at CSA, IISc on architectures for distributed quantum computing and quantum error correction strategies for near-term devices. This work addresses the fundamental challenges of scaling quantum computation beyond single-node systems. Complementing this systems-level research, my undergraduate thesis explores variational quantum algorithms under the guidance of Prof. Kaustav Bhowmick, investigating algorithmic approaches that can leverage near-term quantum hardware despite current limitations.

Beyond quantum computing, I have developed expertise in advanced memory architectures for classical computing systems. This work includes the design of novel memory hierarchies using emerging technologies such as 3D XPoint and memristor-based storage, as well as the development of intelligent in-DRAM prefetching techniques.
